About Dewi Sartika

Childhood and Family Background

Dewi Sartika was born on December 4, 1884, in Bandung, West Java, into a Sundanese noble family. Her parents, Raden Rangga Somanagara and Raden Ayu Raja Permas, were advocates of education, which greatly influenced her early life. Despite living under colonial rule, Dewi Sartika displayed an exceptional interest in education from a young age, showing a passion for teaching and learning even within the confines of traditional norms.

Founding the First Women’s Schooln

In 1904, Dewi Sartika founded the first school for girls in Indonesia, named Sakola Istri (Women’s School), in Bandung. The school was established to provide basic education to women, focusing on reading, writing, sewing, and household skills. This groundbreaking initiative marked the beginning of accessible education for women in Indonesia, inspiring similar movements across the archipelago

Vision for Women’s Empowerment

Dewi Sartika envisioned an Indonesia where women could contribute equally to society. She believed that educating women was key to advancing families and communities. Her school’s curriculum not only taught practical skills but also instilled the value of independence and self-reliance, preparing women to face societal challenges.

Legacy and Recognition

Dewi Sartika’s dedication to women’s education earned her recognition as a national hero in 1966. Her work significantly contributed to the early stages of women’s empowerment in Indonesia. Today, she is celebrated as a pioneer in education who laid the foundation for future generations of women to access knowledge and pursue their potential.

Dewi Sartika’s Lifelong Dedication to Education

Dewi Sartika dedicated her entire life to ensuring that women, regardless of their background, had access to education. Even amidst resistance and challenges, she continued to advocate for her vision of an educated and empowered society. Her perseverance serves as a reminder of the transformative power of education.

Value of Her Life

Education as a Tool for Empowerment

Dewi Sartika firmly believed in education as the foundation of empowerment, particularly for women. She dedicated her life to ensuring that women could gain the skills and knowledge needed to improve their lives and society.

Courage to Lead Change

Sartika’s decision to establish the first women’s school in Indonesia demonstrated her bravery in challenging societal norms and colonial restrictions, making her a role model for future generations.

Perseverance in the Face of Adversity

Despite the difficulties posed by colonial rule and traditional resistance, Dewi Sartika’s resilience helped her achieve her vision for accessible education for all women.

Commitment to Community and Social Responsibility

Dewi Sartika believed in the transformative power of collective progress, emphasizing education not only for personal gain but also for the betterment of families and communities.
